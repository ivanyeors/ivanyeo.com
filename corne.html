My Journey Building a Corne Keyboard

As someone who spends countless hours typing, I decided to embark on a fun DIY adventure to build my own split ergonomic keyboard. This project combines my love for technology, hands-on building, and the satisfaction of creating something uniquely mine.

×

What started as curiosity about mechanical keyboards turned into an exciting weekend project that taught me about electronics, firmware programming, and the joy of building something with my own hands.

Why I Chose the Corne

After diving deep into the mechanical keyboard community and researching various options, the Corne keyboard caught my eye for several reasons:

Perfect for My Setup

The split design lets me position each half exactly where I want, making long coding sessions much more comfortable

Fun to Build

The DIY aspect appealed to my maker spirit - from soldering to programming, every step was a new learning experience

Minimalist Design

With just 36 keys, it challenged me to rethink my typing habits and create efficient layer layouts

My Component Choices

Selecting parts was like putting together a puzzle - each component affecting the final feel and sound of the keyboard.

The Heart of the Build

Went with the classic black PCB and aluminum plates for that premium feel

Tactile Goodness

Chose Boba U4T switches for their satisfying tactile feedback and deep sound profile

Brain Power

Elite-C controllers for their reliability and USB-C convenience

Adventures in ZMK Firmware

Initially started with QMK, but later switched to ZMK for its excellent wireless support and better battery life. The journey of customizing my keyboard layout has been incredibly fun!
